<p><p>The thermophilic dairy starter market comprises two main types: single strain and compound strains. Single strain starters utilize a single microorganism, offering control over fermentation and flavor profiles, ideal for specific cheese varieties. In contrast, compound strain starters combine multiple microorganisms, enhancing complexity in flavor, aroma, and texture. Compound strains are often favored for their synergistic effects, resulting in unique characteristics in products like yogurts and cheeses. Both types contribute significantly to the quality and diversity of dairy products.</p></p>
