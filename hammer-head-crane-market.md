<p><ul><li>Top Slewing</li><li>Bottom Slewing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hammer head crane market is primarily divided into two types: top slewing and bottom slewing cranes. Top slewing cranes feature a horizontal jib that is mounted on a tower, providing greater lifting height and stability, ideal for construction and large-scale projects. In contrast, bottom slewing cranes have their slewing mechanism located at the base, offering flexibility and reduced height requirements, making them suitable for smaller sites or urban environments. Each type serves distinct operational needs in the construction industry.</p></p>
