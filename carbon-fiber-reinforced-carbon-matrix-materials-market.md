<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The carbon fiber reinforced carbon matrix materials market comprises two primary types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process (LIP). CVD involves depositing carbon layers onto a substrate gas, creating a robust composite with superior thermal and mechanical properties, ideal for high-performance applications. In contrast, LIP incorporates liquid resin into a carbon fiber preform, allowing for intricate shapes and improved density. Both methods cater to industries such as aerospace, automotive, and energy, emphasizing strength and thermal stability.</p></p>
